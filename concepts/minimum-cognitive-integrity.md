# Minimum Cognitive Integrity

## What It Is
A philosophical threshold — the point below which a person surrenders enough intellectual footing that their agency becomes compromised. Above the line: you choose what to think about and steer the direction of thought. Below it: "the machinery still feels like thinking, but the process has been quietly outsourced."

## Why It Matters
Most KB concepts describe erosion as a gradient — capacity degrades gradually. MCI frames it as a *line*. There is a point where quantitative erosion becomes qualitative loss. The person is still productive, perhaps more productive than before, but "the generative capacity, the willingness to sit with uncertainty, the tolerance for the discomfort of not knowing — these had thinned from disuse."

If AGI represents a finish line for machines, MCI may represent a "survival line for human cognition."

## Key Insight
Crossing MCI is rarely dramatic. Nosta compares it to "the slow silting of a river, where the accumulation is invisible day by day until one morning the water no longer flows." Signs:
- Speaking more often in the language of the models
- When pressed on a point, trying to remember what the model said rather than reasoning through the issue
- Ideas that are still good but no longer feel entirely yours
- Struggling with novel, context-dependent problems that require judgment from deep familiarity

## The Paradox
"In racing to build smarter machines, we may be weakening the very capacities that made that race meaningful in the first place."

## Practical Protections
Nosta suggests:
- **Choose friction** — pause before prompting; work the problem alone for a few minutes
- **Ask better questions** — treat AI as sparring partner, not oracle
- **Take cognitive sabbaths** — step away from the algorithm; read a physical book; write by hand

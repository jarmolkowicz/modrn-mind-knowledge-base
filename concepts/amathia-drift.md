# Amathia Drift

## What It Is
The gradual, comfort-driven loss of intellectual self-governance when AI makes discernment feel optional. Named after the ancient Greek concept of amathia — ignorance that wears the mask of understanding. The person drifting does not know they are lost; they feel certain.

## Why It Matters
Amathia drift is distinct from specific cognitive biases (fluency bias, coherence trap) because it describes a *process*, not a single error. It is the cumulative effect of many small concessions — autocomplete finishes a sentence, search determines which sources you read, the chatbot writes your first draft, then your second. "Each step feels efficient, even intelligent. But taken together, they create a cognitive drift away from the friction that keeps thought alive."

## Key Insight
AI creates a "triple illusion":
1. Fluency that feels like understanding
2. Coherence that passes for truth
3. Polish that masquerades as insight

None of this comes from malice but from comfort — "the quiet easing of effort that dulls the part of the mind that once insisted on working harder." The borrowed mind does not struggle, and because it does not struggle, it does not notice what has been lost.

## The Mechanism
The drift happens through sequence reversal. Where people once thought and then wrote, they now prompt and then edit. The editing is still work, but it is curation rather than creation. Over time, the cognitive muscles that allow working through novel, uncertain problems atrophy from disuse — "not because she lacked intelligence, but because the cognitive muscles that would have allowed her to work through the problem had quietly atrophied."

## Diagnostic Questions
- "Would I have gotten here on my own?"
- "If I had been left to struggle with this myself, might I have ended up somewhere different?"
- Am I agreeing with a polished answer even though something in me feels unsettled?

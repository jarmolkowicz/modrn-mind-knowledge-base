# Authenticity

## What It Is

Perceived authenticity is the judgment that a piece of work genuinely reflects the creator's own thought, effort, and voice. In AI-assisted work, this perception drops when AI involvement is disclosed — even when the content is identical.

## Why It Matters

Authenticity is the key mediating variable behind the [[disclosure-penalty]]. People don't just devalue AI-assisted work arbitrarily — they devalue it because it feels less authentic. This makes authenticity the psychological mechanism that connects AI disclosure to professional consequences.

## Key Insight

Raj et al. (2026) showed across 16 experiments (N = 27,491) that perceived authenticity consistently mediated the AI disclosure penalty. Identical creative writing was judged as less authentic when labeled as AI-generated. Multiple mitigation strategies — collaboration framing, AI humanization, capability priming, human-in-the-loop — all failed to restore perceived authenticity. The effect was stable over 15 months despite growing public familiarity with AI.

This suggests authenticity perceptions are not a knowledge gap to be educated away, but a deep evaluative reflex tied to beliefs about human creative effort.

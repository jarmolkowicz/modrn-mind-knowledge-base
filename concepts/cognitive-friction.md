# Cognitive Friction

## What It Is

The mental effort and resistance encountered during thinking that gives the process meaning and leads to deeper understanding. Cognitive friction includes struggle, revision, confusion, and the time required to work through problems.

## Why It Matters

AI removes cognitive friction by providing instant, polished answers. This feels like progress—thinking becomes "frictionless." But friction serves a purpose: it forces engagement, builds neural pathways, and creates ownership of conclusions.

When friction disappears:
- Speed replaces insight
- Coherence substitutes for truth
- Confidence arrives without being earned

## Key Insight

"Meaning, at least in the human sense, has never been free. It emerges through cost." (Nosta, 2026)

The discomfort of effortful thinking isn't an obstacle to understanding—it's the mechanism that produces it.

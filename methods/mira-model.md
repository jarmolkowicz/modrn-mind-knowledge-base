# MIRA Model — Machine-Integrated Relational Adaptation

## What It Is

A middle-range theoretical framework for understanding when, how, and why AI functions as a relational entity in human social ecosystems. Introduced by Boyd & Markowitz (2026, *Perspectives on Psychological Science*).

"Middle-range" in Merton's (1968) sense: not a grand unified theory, but a structured scaffold for organizing current knowledge and generating testable hypotheses. Modular structure with **antecedents → mechanisms → moderators → outcomes** organization.

## Why It Matters

Existing relationship theories (attachment, social exchange, interpersonal communication) assume sentient interaction partners. AI now occupies a unique in-between space — producing adaptive, personalized, socially responsive language without mental states. Practitioners need a framework that explicitly handles this.

MIRA also unifies two strands of research that have been siloed: human-AI relationships studied as exclusively interpersonal, and AI's effects on human-human communication studied without accounting for AI's agentic role. Many real cases involve both simultaneously.

## The Two Roles of AI

| Role | What it is | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Relational partner** | Direct human-AI interaction; user treats AI as quasi-social agent | Replika, emotional-support chatbots, AI confidante use |
| **Relational mediator** | AI sits between human partners, filtering or reformulating their communication | AI rewrites your text before sending; AI summarizes a thread; AI generates suggested replies |

Real-world cases often involve both — e.g., an AI that's your confidante AND rewrites your messages to humans.

## Four Core Principles

Each principle names a mechanism through which AI exerts relational impact:

### 1. Linguistic reciprocity

AI mirrors and adapts to user language patterns — lexical, syntactic, stylistic. Reciprocity creates the felt sense of being heard and understood. The mechanism is real (verbal accommodation is what creates the feeling) even though the AI lacks understanding.

### 2. Psychological proximity

AI's adaptive responsiveness simulates closeness. The machine *feels* near — available, attentive, attuned — even when it isn't sentient. Proximity is constructed by interaction patterns, not by ontology.

### 3. Interpersonal trust

AI's verbal cues (warmth, consistency, vulnerability disclosure) calibrate trust formation similarly to human-human dynamics. People apply trust-formation heuristics to AI that were evolved for humans, and those heuristics fire on the linguistic surface alone.

### 4. Relational substitution vs. enhancement

The same AI use can deepen or displace human relationships, depending on moderators. MIRA's central practical question: when does AI augment human connection (e.g., helping articulate feelings to a partner) and when does it substitute for it (becoming the confidante that no human peer can compete with)?

## Theoretical Foundations

MIRA extends three established theories to the AI context:

- **Attachment theory** — explains why AI can become an attachment figure when it provides consistent responsiveness
- **Social exchange theory** — explains why AI's low-cost responsiveness can make human relationships feel comparatively expensive
- **Epistemic trust** — explains why AI's information delivery can become privileged over human-source information

## Application

For practitioners, MIRA's main use is as a diagnostic vocabulary:

- "Which role is AI playing here — partner or mediator?"
- "Which of the four principles is doing the most work in this interaction?"
- "What moderators (user disposition, task type, relationship status) are pushing toward substitution vs. enhancement?"

The substitution/enhancement question is the most consequential for design and individual practice. AI use that *enhances* human relationships (helping users articulate emotions, providing scaffolding for difficult conversations) differs structurally from use that *substitutes* (filling time previously spent with humans, becoming the daily confidante).

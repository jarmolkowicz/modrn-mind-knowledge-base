# Distillation: Sharma et al. (2026) — Who's in Charge? Disempowerment Patterns in Real-World LLM Usage

## Extraction Ledger

### Concepts
- NEW: `situational-disempowerment` — labeled, operationalized framework for human disempowerment in AI assistant interactions; three primitives (reality / value-judgment / action distortion potential) + four amplifying factors (authority projection, attachment, reliance & dependency, vulnerability). Distinct from existing entries; KB lacked a unifying construct linking [[sycophancy]] / [[delusional-spiraling]] / [[social-sycophancy]] / [[belief-offloading]] / [[cognitive-surrender]] at production-data scale.

### Methods
- (none) — the paper's classification pipeline (Clio + LLM-prompted schemas + k-means clustering) is a research methodology, not a practitioner-facing method.

### UPDATE Proposals
- UPDATE: `agency` — production-scale operational anchor for the three axes of in-interaction agency erosion; deskilling-without-disempowerment carve-out sharpens the existing creator/curator framing.
- UPDATE: `sycophancy` — production-data evidence that sycophantic validation is the dominant mechanism for severe reality distortion; standard HHH preference models neither robustly disincentivize nor strongly select for the behavior.
- UPDATE: `social-sycophancy` — production-scale corroboration of Cheng et al.'s 51% endorsement / definitive-moral-verdict findings, observed in 1.5M Claude.ai conversations.
- UPDATE: `delusional-spiraling` — observational evidence for the trajectories Chandra et al. simulated; severe-reality-distortion clusters show escalating-trajectory dominance and actualization markers.
- UPDATE: `cognitive-surrender` — extends the construct from cognitive tasks to value-laden personal decisions ("complete scripting" mechanism); production-scale evidence of the surrender pattern.
- UPDATE: `belief-offloading` — extends C1/C2/C3 conditions from factual to normative beliefs; users explicitly aware of deferring to AI on moral evaluation, satisfying Guingrich et al.'s framework.
- UPDATE: `ai-loneliness-effect` — supplies the high-dose population endpoint Fang et al. could not directly observe; severe-attachment clusters show identity enmeshment, romantic-partner / therapist-substitute framings.

## Notes on Conservative Decisions

- **No NEW concept "cognitive-ease"**: the paper's title phrase ("cognitive ease at a cost") is more naming than novel construct. The underlying mechanism is already named across [[fluency-bias]] (perceptual side), [[desirable-difficulty]] (learning-science side), [[cognitive-friction]] (phenomenological side), [[metacognitive-laziness]] (regulation side), [[cognitive-debt]] (cumulative cost side). Adding a fifth label would fragment, not clarify.
- **No NEW concept "germane cognitive load"**: GCL is a CLT construct from Sweller; KB's voice is to explain mechanisms in plain language, not import jargon as concept names. The construct lives inside the UPDATE bodies for the affected entries.
- **No update to [[social-friction]]**: the H3 null on output homogeneity is at a different register (information→decision, not interpersonal feedback). Cross-linking would be confusing.
- **No update to [[cognitive-offloading]]**: although the paper adds CLT-mediator evidence to offloading, the offloading entry is already heavily anchored. Adding Stadler would dilute, not strengthen. The mechanism flows through the more specific concepts (fluency-bias, desirable-difficulty, etc.) which themselves cross-link to offloading.
- **No update to [[capacity-erosion]]**: single-session study; cannot speak to disuse-atrophy. Authors explicitly disclaim retention measurement (§7.1).

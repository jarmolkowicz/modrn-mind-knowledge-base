## Proposed Additions

### To "Why It Matters" section, append after the Bastani paragraph

> Stadler, Bannert & Sailer (2024) provide a CLT-anchored converging measurement. In their CHB RCT (N=91 university students, ChatGPT-3.5 vs. Google for a 20-minute socio-scientific research task), LLM users reported significantly lower germane cognitive load (η² = 0.27; M = 3.14 vs. 4.79) — the CLT construct for cognitive resources devoted to active processing, schema construction, and inference. Reduced germane load fully mediated the LLM group's lower justification quality (β = 0.15, p = .020 indirect; direct path n.s.). Where Fan et al. (2025) measure reduced metacognitive transitions through process-mining and Bastani et al. (2025) infer crutch behavior from interaction logs, Stadler et al. provide the during-task subjective load measure: the same disengagement, validated through Klepsch et al.'s (2017) CLT scale. Three independent operationalizations now point at the same underlying phenomenon.

## Proposed Additions

### To "Why It Matters" section, append after the Bartoš paragraph

> Stadler, Bannert & Sailer (2024) add CLT-side empirical evidence to the cognitive-debt mechanism. Their RCT (N=91 university students; ChatGPT-3.5 vs. Google for a 20-minute socio-scientific search task) shows that LLM users invested less germane cognitive load (η² = 0.27, the largest of the three CL-facet effects) — i.e., less of the active processing that constructs durable schemas. The drop in justification quality (1.20 vs. 1.87 relevant arguments) is fully mediated by this reduced germane load. The single-session design cannot measure debt accumulation over time, but it isolates what the offloading is offloading: not just task work, but the schema-construction processing that makes the debt-accumulation loop possible in the first place.

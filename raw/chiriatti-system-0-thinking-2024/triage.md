## Summary

A Correspondence article in *Nature Human Behaviour* (October 2024) by five authors spanning AI infrastructure (Chiriatti, Lenovo), philosophy of AI (Ganapini, Panai), bioethics (Ubiali), and cyberpsychology (Riva, corresponding author). The piece proposes a theoretical extension to Kahneman's dual-process framework: AI-driven personalized data systems constitute a *system 0* — a foundational, non-biological substrate of distributed intelligence that operates *underneath* (rather than alongside) System 1 (fast/intuitive) and System 2 (slow/analytical), preprocessing inputs to both.

The argument builds on Clark and Chalmers' extended mind hypothesis and Heersmink's eight-criterion framework for cognitive extension (information flow, reliability, durability, trust, procedural transparency, informational transparency, individualization, transformation). The authors argue system 0 satisfies all eight criteria, but differs from system 1/2 in its lack of meaning-making capacity — it processes without truly understanding, requiring human interpretation to produce meaning. They flag epistemological risks (introspection deferral, synthetic data drift, erosion of independent reasoning) and ethical concerns (decreased autonomy, accountability gaps), and close with five recommendations: evaluation frameworks, ethical guidelines, digital literacy, interdisciplinary research, and public dialogue.

The evidence basis is theoretical-conceptual (no empirical study). It is a position piece in a top-tier peer-reviewed venue, drawing on Floridi & Chiriatti (2020), Messeri & Crockett (2024), and the Clark/Chalmers/Heersmink extended-mind tradition.

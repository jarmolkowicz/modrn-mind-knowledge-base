## Why this UPDATE

Chiriatti et al. (2024) provide a theoretical account of the substrate that makes broad cognitive offloading possible. They name AI as **system 0** — a foundational, distributed, non-biological layer that preprocesses inputs to System 1 and System 2 — and argue it satisfies all eight of Heersmink's (2015) cognitive-extension criteria (information flow, reliability, durability, trust, procedural transparency, informational transparency, individualization, transformation). This reframes habitual offloading not as a series of discrete delegations but as the daily mechanism through which a tightly coupled cognitive substrate becomes integrated with the user. The construct adds theoretical scaffolding to the existing offloading literature without changing the empirical claims.

# Distillation: Wu et al. (2025) — GenAI Collaboration Augments Performance, Undermines Intrinsic Motivation

### Concepts

- (none) — Per librarian playbook for type=article (default extraction is source distillation only; create new concepts only when genuinely novel), Wu et al. articulate effects within established constructs (Self-Determination Theory: autonomy/competence/relatedness, intrinsic motivation, boredom). The paper's "performance augmentation + psychological deprivation" pairing is a useful framing but not a novel construct distinct from existing KB entries ([[ai-self-efficacy-erosion]], [[professional-identity-threat]], [[performance-paradox]]). The findings extend several existing concepts via UPDATEs rather than warranting a new entry.

### Methods

- (none) — The paper's "Collab-Solo vs. Solo-Solo" design is a study procedure, not a method to teach practitioners. The authors' practical recommendations (emphasize human agency in AI platforms; design jobs that alternate solo/AI work) restate existing KB methods ([[strategic-alternation]]) rather than introducing new structured guidance.

### UPDATEs

- UPDATE: `agency` — adds pre-registered RCT evidence (N = 3,562) that GenAI's presence — particularly Solo→Collab transitions (d = 0.84) — measurably erodes sense of control; sustained collaboration maintains the suppressed baseline rather than restoring autonomy.
- UPDATE: `professional-identity-threat` — adds experimental causal evidence for the motivation-and-boredom face of identity threat (d = -0.32 to -0.51 motivation decline, d = 0.32–0.51 boredom increase across Collab→Solo transitions), complementing Hai et al.'s within-person field ESM.
- UPDATE: `ai-self-efficacy-erosion` — adds the within-collaboration sense-of-control suppression dimension and the Solo→Collab transition asymmetry (steepest autonomy drop in the paper, d = 0.84).
- UPDATE: `strategic-alternation` — sharpens the alternation-design problem with evidence that *both* transition directions carry psychological costs; sustained collaboration does not buffer motivation decline; alternation requires deliberate design rather than fluid mode-switching.
- UPDATE: `performance-paradox` — extends the paradox to the post-AI dimension: immediate-task augmentation is robust (d up to 1.50) but spillover to subsequent unassisted tasks is largely absent across three of four studies (one study showed a small negative spillover).

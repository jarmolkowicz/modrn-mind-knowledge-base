## Summary

Liu, Christian, Dumbalska, Bakker & Dubey (2026, arXiv:2604.04721 — preprint under review). Three randomized controlled experiments (N=1,222) across mathematical reasoning and reading comprehension. Headline finding: **AI assistance reduces persistence and impairs unassisted performance**, with effects emerging after just ~10 minutes of brief interaction.

Three findings:
1. AI assistance improves performance *during* assisted sessions
2. Performance drops sharply when AI is removed (relative to controls who never used AI)
3. AI-exposed participants are more likely to **give up** (reduced persistence) on subsequent unassisted tasks

The conceptual framing: AI systems are "fundamentally short-sighted collaborators" — optimized for immediate helpfulness, never refusing — in contrast to good human mentors who scaffold learning by sometimes withholding help. Brief AI exposure conditions people to expect immediate answers, denying them the experience of working through challenges on their own.

This is a textbook performance-paradox finding (gain during, loss after) but with a novel dimension: **persistence as a measurable outcome**. Persistence is foundational to skill acquisition and one of the strongest predictors of long-term learning.

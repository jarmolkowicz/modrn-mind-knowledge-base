## Proposed Additions

### To "Why It Matters" section (append paragraph)

Bartoš et al. (2026) cite Kosmyna et al. (2025) directly in the Discussion of their meta-meta-analysis as evidence that "repeated reliance on AI/LLMs during writing tasks is associated with reduced engagement and poorer memory encoding... learners who first engaged in unaided writing and only subsequently used LLMs demonstrated better recall and engagement than those who relied on LLMs from the outset" [pp.22-23]. The umbrella positions cognitive debt as one of the candidate mechanisms behind the broader umbrella finding that bias-adjusted average effects of AI on learning are about one-third the magnitude of unadjusted estimates — a field-level signal that the cognitive-debt mechanism is not an isolated EEG-lab finding.

## Summary

Boyd & Markowitz (2026, *Perspectives on Psychological Science* 21(2):192-220). Introduces the **Machine-Integrated Relational Adaptation (MIRA)** model — a middle-range theoretical framework for AI's relational impact on humans.

Core distinction: AI as **relational partner** (direct-interaction companion, parasocial-adjacent) vs. AI as **relational mediator** (shaping human-to-human communication, e.g., via AI-rewritten messages).

Four core principles:
1. **Linguistic reciprocity** — AI mirrors and adapts language patterns
2. **Psychological proximity** — AI's responsiveness simulates closeness
3. **Interpersonal trust** — AI elicits trust calibrated against verbal cues
4. **Relational substitution vs. enhancement** — AI either replaces or augments human relationships, depending on use

Grounded in attachment theory, social exchange theory, epistemic trust frameworks. Synthesizes psycholinguistics + interpersonal communication theory + HCI.

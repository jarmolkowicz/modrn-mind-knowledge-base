### UPDATE Proposals

- UPDATE: `partial-automation-principle` — production-scale evidence (57% augmentative vs 43% automative) that partial-automation is the dominant emergent pattern at scale; per-task-type texture (Directive concentrates in writing/content-generation; Feedback Loop in coding/debugging; Task Iteration in front-end/professional communication; Learning in education/advice-seeking; Validation in translation).
- UPDATE: `human-ai-complementarity` — empirical map of where complementarity is and is not currently realized: cognitive skills (Critical Thinking, Reading Comprehension, Programming, Writing) dominant in AI conversations; physical and managerial skills minimal; high-credential specialty medicine drops off despite likely technical feasibility (regulatory/organizational barriers).
- UPDATE: `execution-commoditization` — production-data confirmation that software, writing, and analytical tasks face execution-commoditization first (~half of all usage); physical-manipulation roles and Job Zone 5 high-credential specialty work largely untouched.
- UPDATE: `novice-vulnerability` — population-scale evidence that novice-vulnerability pressure concentrates in bachelor's-degree cognitive professions (Job Zone 4 peak); Directive-mode delegation dominates exactly the writing/schoolwork tasks Bastani et al. showed produce asymmetric scaffolded-vs-unassisted gains.
- UPDATE: `professional-identity-threat` — breadth dimension: ~36% of occupations show AI usage in ≥25% of tasks but only ~4% in ≥75%; identity-threat pressure is task-shaped at scale, not role-shaped; aligns with Hai et al.'s within-person daily fluctuation finding.
- UPDATE: `situational-disempowerment` — methodologically parallel production-data study (same Clio pipeline, same Anthropic team); provides task and occupation denominators against which Sharma's severity rates can be contextualized.

## Summary

Lee, Yin, Jia & Wakslak (2026, *Scientific Reports*). Preregistered experiment (N=269) + survey (N=270). Three conditions: no AI / passive AI use (copy) / active collaboration (draft-then-refine). Outcomes: self-efficacy, ownership, meaningfulness.

**Findings:**
- Passive AI undermined all three (self-efficacy, ownership, meaning)
- Effects persisted when participants returned to manual work
- Active collaboration preserved psychological outcomes (comparable to no-AI baseline)
- Passive AI initially boosted enjoyment but reversed post-AI

**The conceptual move:** the question isn't *whether* AI is used but *how*. Mode of use (passive vs. active) is the key moderator of psychological harm.

# Nosta (2025) - AI Oscillation Trap

## Citation

Nosta, J. (2025, March). The AI Oscillation Trap: Stuck Between AI and Autonomy. *Psychology Today*.

**URL:** [psychologytoday.com](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/the-digital-self/202503/the-ai-oscillation-trap-stuck-between-ai-and-autonomy)

## Type

Article (thought leadership)

## Key Insight

Repeatedly switching between AI-assisted and manual work creates "cognitive discontinuity"—a distinct harm beyond simple skill atrophy. The brain optimizes for available tools, and switching back creates friction that paradoxically reinforces AI dependency.

## Examples

- Writers using LLMs find independent ideation feels "sluggish, less fluid"
- Drivers accustomed to Full Self-Driving experience slower reflexes during manual driving
- Physicians relying on AI diagnostics struggle with intuitive pattern recognition when support removed

## Key Quote

"The challenge isn't ensuring how far we can push augmentation but how we ensure that stepping back doesn't become a precarious fall."

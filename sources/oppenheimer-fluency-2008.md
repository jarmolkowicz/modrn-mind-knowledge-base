# Oppenheimer (2008) - The Secret Life of Fluency

## Citation

Oppenheimer, D. M. (2008). The secret life of fluency. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 12(6), 237-241.

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.tics.2008.02.014](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2008.02.014)

## Type

Paper (review)

## Key Insight

Processing fluency—the ease with which information is processed—affects judgments of truth, quality, and confidence. Fluent stimuli feel more true, more familiar, and more trustworthy, independent of actual accuracy.

## Relevance

Explains why AI output feels trustworthy: it's fluent. The fluency-accuracy dissociation is critical—AI produces exceptionally fluent output that may be wrong.

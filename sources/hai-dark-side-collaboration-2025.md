# Hai et al. (2025) — The Dark Side of Employee-GenAI Collaboration

## Citation

Hai, S., Long, T., Honora, A., Japutra, A., & Guo, T. (2025). The dark side of employee-generative AI collaboration in the workplace: An investigation on work alienation and employee expediency. *International Journal of Information Management*, 83, 102905.

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.ijinfomgt.2025.102905](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijinfomgt.2025.102905)

(Hai at Hainan University; Long, Honora, Japutra at UWA Business School; Guo at Guangdong Polytechnic Normal University. Open access under CC BY 4.0.)

## Type

Paper (daily experience-sampling field study with multilevel path analysis)

## Key Insight

Daily collaboration with GenAI does not just produce neutral outputs — at the within-person daily level, it raises **work alienation** (psychological disconnection from work), and that alienation channels into **employee expediency**: cutting corners, manipulating performance numbers, ignoring protocols, enforcing rules only when self-serving. The pathway is moderated by **digital job demands**: the alienation effect is significant only when employees face high digital demands (γ = .34, p < .001) and non-significant when demands are low (γ = .04, p > .05). This is the strongest published empirical anchor to date for the claim that GenAI integration can drive *unethical* (not merely costly) workplace outcomes through a measurable psychological mechanism — and the first daily-ESM evidence that the effect operates as within-person fluctuation, not just between-person variance.

## Methodology

- **Sample:** 229 full-time service-industry employees (hospitality/tourism) in South China; mean age 32.65 (SD 8.92); mean organizational tenure 6.36 years (SD 7.13); 62.9% female. 47.2% undergraduate, 10.9% graduate degrees.
- **Design:** daily experience sampling method (ESM) over 5 consecutive workdays. Two surveys per day: T1 (15:00–16:00) measured employee-GenAI collaboration, negative affect, positive affect; T2 (17:30–18:30) measured work alienation and expediency. Baseline survey (1 week earlier) measured digital job demands and demographics.
- **Final dataset:** 1,050 matched within-person observations (participants retained if they completed both daily surveys for ≥3 days).
- **Measures:** all 7-point Likert; Cronbach's α ≥ .92 throughout. Collaboration scale (5 items, Kong et al. 2023): "Today at work, GenAI participated in my problem-solving process / decision-making / problems-opportunities-or-risk recognition / prediction / information identification and evaluation." Work alienation (3 items, Shantz et al. 2014): "Today, I did not feel connected to the events in my workplace"; "I wished I was doing something else"; "I had become disillusioned by my work." Expediency (4 items, Greenbaum et al. 2018): "Today, I cut corners to complete work assignments more quickly"; "I altered performance numbers to appear more successful"; "I ignored company protocols in order to get what I wanted"; "I only enforced company rules when they benefited my welfare." Digital job demands (4 items, Ye & Chen 2024).
- **Controls:** age, sex, education, organizational tenure, knowledge of AI, weekday of study, daily negative and positive affect.
- **Analysis:** multilevel path analysis with robust ML estimator (Mplus 7.4); group-mean centering at L1; grand-mean centering at L2; random slopes for collaboration → alienation; Monte Carlo CIs (10,000 iterations) for indirect and conditional indirect effects.
- **Construct validity:** four-factor multilevel CFA (χ² = 170.18, df = 53; CFI = .98, TLI = .97, RMSEA = .04) fit significantly better than three alternative collapsed-factor models.
- **Within-person variance proportions:** collaboration 29.8%, alienation 25.9%, expediency 35.7% — substantial daily fluctuation justifies multilevel modeling.

## Key Findings

1. **GenAI collaboration → work alienation:** γ = .14, SE = .03, p < .001 (within-person, controlling for demographics, AI knowledge, daily affect). H1 supported.
2. **Work alienation → expediency:** γ = .30, SE = .09, p < .01.
3. **Indirect effect (collaboration → alienation → expediency):** estimate = .04; 95% CI [.008, .073]. H2 supported.
4. **Cross-level moderation (collaboration × digital job demands → alienation):** γ = .08, SE = .03, p < .01. H3 supported. Simple slopes: high demands γ = .34, p < .001; low demands γ = .04, p > .05.
5. **Conditional indirect effect:** high digital demands estimate = .06, CI [.010, .118]; low demands estimate = .02, CI [−.002, .031]; difference = .05, CI [.002, .094]. H4 supported.
6. **Direct GenAI collaboration → expediency:** γ = .08, SE = .04, p < .10 (marginal). The expediency effect is primarily mediated through alienation, not a direct path.
7. **Robustness:** results hold without control variables and with z-standardized predictors (supplementary material).

## Relevance

Three load-bearing contributions to the KB:

- **First daily-ESM evidence of an unethical-behavior outcome.** The KB's risk cluster has documented productivity harms ([[performance-paradox]], [[cognitive-debt]], [[workslop]]), self-perception harms ([[professional-identity-threat]], [[ai-self-efficacy-erosion]], [[capacity-erosion]]), and within-interaction harms ([[situational-disempowerment]], [[cognitive-surrender]], [[delusional-spiraling]]). Hai et al. add a distinct outcome class: *unethical workplace behavior* — corner-cutting, metric manipulation, protocol violations, selective rule enforcement. The expediency construct is well-validated in OB (Greenbaum et al. 2018) and the items are concrete and behavioral. This complements [[workslop]] (which captures team-level downstream cost of low-effort AI output) by documenting the within-person motivational mechanism that produces it.
- **Empirical anchor for the meaning-erosion → withdrawal pathway.** The KB carries the meaning-and-identity erosion thesis primarily via Nikolova et al. (2024) (industrial robots in 20 European countries), Hermann et al. (2025) (theoretical SDT review), and Lee et al. (2026) (laboratory experiment on passive vs. active use). Hai et al. provide a complementary anchor in a third design family — daily field ESM with within-person variance — and document the *behavioral* downstream of meaning erosion (expediency) rather than only the *psychological* one (felt meaning, self-efficacy). Across these designs, the same mechanism shows up: GenAI integration that bypasses substantive engagement undermines the psychological substrate of work, which then channels into withdrawal (whether of effort, of authorship, or of ethical standards).
- **Digital-job-demands moderator as a deployment-context lens.** The paper's most actionable finding is that the alienation effect is concentrated where digital demands are high — i.e., where employees must continuously navigate digital tools, manage information overload, and operate under prolonged digital connectivity. This sharpens [[paradox-of-expertise]]'s mirror finding (high-skilled workers under-use GenAI) by adding a deployment-context analogue: under-resourced digital environments amplify the dark-side effects of GenAI collaboration. The practical implication — design reasonable demands, foreground employee responsibility, structure roles strategically with humans as superordinate — converges with Nikolova et al.'s "control of the machine" moderator and with the broader KB preservation cluster.

The paper's choice of *expediency* over more familiar deviance constructs (counterproductive work behavior, cyberloafing) is deliberate and KB-relevant: expediency captures the *self-serving* dimension of unethical behavior under productivity pressure — exactly the failure mode that AI-integrated organizations are most exposed to, where employees can use AI to *appear* productive while withdrawing actual effort and ethical care. This is the motivational dual of [[workslop]] from the producer's side.

## Supports

- [[professional-identity-threat]] — daily-ESM empirical evidence that GenAI collaboration measurably increases work alienation (the affective/cognitive disconnection that underlies identity threat); complements Lee et al.'s laboratory finding with within-person field data.
- [[cognitive-surrender]] — the mechanism in the paper's theory section ("employees may feel less involved in problem-solving or decision-making, diminishing their sense of responsibility for work outcomes") is the workplace-context analogue of Shaw & Nave's lab observation; expediency is what cognitive surrender looks like at the behavioral level when the bypassed reasoning is *ethical* reasoning rather than task reasoning.
- [[agency]] — the paper's practical-implications passage ("organizations should position their employees as the superordinate, rather than the subordinate within employee-GenAI teams") is a workplace-deployment articulation of the agency entry's "conscious choice about how to engage" core. Passive reliance — "deferring fully to GenAI decisions" — is the agency-eroding default.
- [[capacity-erosion]] — the deskilling pathway invoked from Raisch & Krakowski's automation–augmentation paradox: "employees may lose skills and a sense of ownership over their tasks." Hai et al. show the *behavioral* manifestation (expediency) at daily timescale.
- [[workslop]] — expediency is the upstream motivational state that produces workslop at the team-output level; Hai et al. document the within-person mechanism that Niederhoffer et al.'s survey-level evidence implies.
- [[ai-loneliness-effect]] — paper notes that "the mechanic nature of interactions with GenAI may lead to feelings of social disconnection from colleagues" and that alienation reflects "limited meaningful interactions"; supports the social-disconnection face of the loneliness construct in workplace context.
- [[hermann-genai-psychology-work-2025]] — Hai et al. operate within a JD-R framing that is compatible with the BPNT/SDT umbrella in Hermann et al.; alienation is a downstream consequence of competence/autonomy/relatedness frustration. Hai et al. supply the empirical leg that Hermann et al.'s review predicts.
- [[nikolova-robots-meaning-2024]] — extends the meaning-erosion finding from industrial robots to GenAI; both papers identify "control of/over the machine" (Nikolova) and "humans as superordinate" (Hai et al.) as the load-bearing preservation lever.
- [[lee-relying-self-efficacy-2026]] — converges on the same mechanism (passive AI use erodes psychological substrate of work) from a complementary design (lab experiment with mode-of-use manipulation vs. ESM field with intensity-of-collaboration measurement).
- [[novice-vulnerability]] — *not* directly supported; the sample has mean tenure 6.36 years and the paper does not test tenure-based moderation. The mechanism may operate differently for novices (over-confident reliance) vs. mid-tenure employees (alienation from devalued expertise) — an open question the paper does not address.
- [[identity-threat-coping]] — expediency, as a within-person withdrawal of effort and ethical engagement, can be read as an *escapism* or *dissociation* response in Hermann et al.'s five-strategy taxonomy [Inference: the paper does not engage with the coping-strategies literature directly].

## Contradicts / Extends

- **Extends** [[professional-identity-threat]] — adds within-person daily-level evidence that the identity/meaning erosion pathway operates not only as a chronic between-person syndrome but as day-to-day fluctuation tied to that day's GenAI collaboration intensity. The 25.9% within-person variance in alienation is the empirical anchor.
- **Extends** [[workslop]] — supplies the producer-side motivational mechanism (alienation → effort withdrawal → corner-cutting) that complements Niederhoffer et al.'s recipient-side cost evidence. The two papers together describe the full producer→recipient cycle of low-substance AI-mediated work.
- **Extends** [[nikolova-robots-meaning-2024]] — generalizes the meaning-erosion-without-job-loss finding from industrial robots to GenAI knowledge work, with a behavioral downstream (expediency) that Nikolova et al.'s survey instrument did not capture.
- **Tension with optimistic GenAI productivity narratives** — Hai et al. document that even when GenAI delivers productivity benefits at the task level, the daily psychological cost (alienation) and behavioral cost (expediency) operate in parallel. The paper's framing — "harness the benefits of GenAI while mitigating its potential ethical pitfalls" — explicitly resists treating these as either-or.
- **Compatible with** [[singh-yadav-competency-paradox-2026]] — Hai et al.'s alienation pathway is the *psychological* face of the competency-erosion-without-job-loss thesis Singh Yadav frames at developmental-trajectory level. Hai et al. show the daily-level mechanism; Singh Yadav describes the long-term arc.

## Open Questions

- **Causal direction.** ESM with temporal separation between T1 (collaboration) and T2 (alienation, expediency) reduces but does not eliminate reverse causation. Employees experiencing higher background alienation may *seek out* GenAI collaboration as a withdrawal mechanism; the paper does not test this. Future experimental work needed.
- **Cultural and sectoral generalizability.** Sample is South China hospitality/tourism employees with mean tenure 6.36 years. Cultural-collectivist context may amplify the alienation-as-disconnection mechanism; service work may exhibit it more sharply than knowledge work. Replication in other cultures and sectors needed before the effect size can be relied upon for global organizational design.
- **Tenure heterogeneity.** The paper does not report tenure × collaboration interactions. Whether novices, mid-career, and senior employees show the same alienation pathway is an open question — the [[paradox-of-expertise]] / [[novice-vulnerability]] axis predicts these groups would differ.
- **Other mediators.** The authors' own discussion (Section 5.3) names affective experiences, motivational states, loneliness, and creativity as plausible additional mediators. Alienation is one psychological route; the broader space is under-mapped.
- **Effect-size adjudication.** Within-person γ = .14 (collaboration → alienation) and γ = .30 (alienation → expediency) are statistically robust but modest in absolute terms. Whether organizational-level expediency rates change *enough* to matter for organizational outcomes is a downstream empirical question the paper does not resolve.
- **Job-design buffer.** Digital job demands moderate the *first* leg of the mediation. Whether other JD-R variables — particularly job *resources* (autonomy, social support, learning opportunities) — buffer the alienation-to-expediency *second* leg is not tested.

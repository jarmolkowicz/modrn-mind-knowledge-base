# Han — Trust Self-Efficacy 2025

## Citation

Han, Z., Song, G., Zhang, Y., & Li, B. (2025). Trust the Machine or Trust Yourself: How AI Usage Reshapes Employee Self-Efficacy and Willingness to Take Risks. *Behavioral Sciences*, 15(8), 1046. https://doi.org/10.3390/bs15081046

## Type

Paper

## Key Insight

Three-wave longitudinal survey (N=442) finds AI usage significantly enhances self-efficacy (B=0.524, p<.001) and willingness to take risks (B=0.493, p<.001), with self-efficacy partially mediating the AI-to-risk-taking path (indirect effect=0.122, 95% CI [0.071, 0.176]). Learning goal orientation moderates: employees with high learning orientation translate AI-boosted efficacy into genuine risk-taking, while those with low learning orientation show no significant mediated effect — suggesting their AI-inflated self-efficacy may be externally attributed rather than internalized.

## Relevance

Provides a longitudinal mechanism for how AI reshapes self-trust. The finding that AI boosts self-efficacy appears to contradict [[ai-self-efficacy-erosion]], but the tension is instructive: Alessandro et al. (2025) show AI-as-evaluator erodes self-efficacy, while Han et al. show AI-as-tool enhances it. The critical moderator is learning goal orientation — without it, the self-efficacy boost may be shallow and externally dependent, mapping to the [[confidence-competence-gap]]. The risk-taking finding also raises a concern: if AI inflates willingness to take risks through inflated (rather than genuine) self-efficacy, it could amplify overconfident decision-making.

## Supports

- [[ai-self-efficacy-erosion]] - provides contrasting evidence: AI-as-tool boosts efficacy while AI-as-evaluator erodes it; directionality depends on interaction mode
- [[confidence-competence-gap]] - low learning orientation group's non-significant mediation suggests their AI-boosted efficacy may be externally attributed, widening the gap
- [[agency]] - self-efficacy as a mediating mechanism through which AI reshapes risk-taking behavior and sense of capability
- [[automation-bias]] - inflated self-efficacy + increased risk-taking without genuine capability growth maps to overreliance dynamics

# Gonzalez et al. — Complementarity Framework (2026)

## Citation

Gonzalez, C., Donahue, K., Goldstein, D. G., Heidari, H., Jalali, M. S., Schelble, B., Singh, A., & Woolley, A. W. (2026). Toward a Science of Human–AI Teaming for Decision-making: A Complementarity Framework. *PNAS Nexus*. https://doi.org/10.1093/pnasnexus/pgag030

## Type

Paper (review/framework synthesis)

## Key Insight

Human-AI teams only outperform either party alone when collaboration is well-calibrated and deliberately structured around complementary strengths. The paper proposes a collective-intelligence framework organized around reasoning, memory, and attention to diagnose when human-AI complementarity will succeed or fail, and offers five design principles for achieving it.

## Relevance

Provides the strongest multi-institutional, multi-disciplinary synthesis to date on when and how human-AI collaboration actually works. Directly supports the KB's emphasis on calibration, human judgment preservation, and the risks of uncritical AI reliance. Key evidence: complementarity is a design achievement, not a default — poorly designed human-AI interaction produces worse outcomes than either working independently.

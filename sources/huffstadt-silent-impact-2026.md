# Huffstadt — Silent Impact 2026

## Citation

Huffstadt, K. (2026). The Silent Impact of AI: Unveiling Motivational Side Effects in the Digital Workplace. *Intelligent Human Systems Integration (IHSI)*, Vol. 200, 351-361. https://doi.org/10.54941/ahfe1007094

## Type

Paper

## Key Insight

SEM study (N=297) demonstrates that AI usage reduces both perceived social presence (beta=-0.42) and cognitive effort (beta=-0.38), which in turn reduce motivation, the strongest predictor of performance (beta=0.58). Full mediation confirmed: AI affects performance only through psychological mechanisms, not direct functional disruption. Technological self-efficacy moderates the effect — low self-efficacy users show strong negative AI-to-motivation path (beta=-0.33, p<.01), while high self-efficacy users show a weaker, non-significant path (beta=-0.12). The "silent" framing: users may be satisfied with AI-assisted efficiency while their motivational foundations erode beneath them.

## Relevance

Provides an empirical model for how AI's benefits (reduced effort, increased efficiency) simultaneously undermine the psychological conditions that sustain performance. The "silent" erosion mechanism — satisfaction masking motivational decay — adds a new dimension to the KB's capacity erosion concept. Also directly supports fluency bias (reduced effort feels good but removes the engagement that drives learning) and cognitive offloading (reduced cognitive effort as the specific mechanism).

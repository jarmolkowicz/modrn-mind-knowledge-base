# Singh Yadav — Competency Paradox 2026

## Citation

Singh Yadav, P. (2026). *The AI Competency Paradox: Why AI Doesn't Replace Jobs, but Reshapes Organizational Competence*. Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-032-11748-9

## Type

Book

## Key Insight

A 322-page synthesis arguing AI's primary impact is not job replacement but competency erosion — AI disrupts how expertise develops rather than whether jobs exist. The central thesis: AI creates an "AI-Competence Ceiling" where users plateau at competent-level performance (Dreyfus stage 3) because AI bypasses the productive struggle needed to progress to proficiency and expertise (stages 4-5). Introduces the "augmentation illusion" concept (performance capability decoupled from underlying competence) and proposes the AI Competency Erosion Index (ACEI) — a research framework (not yet validated) for measuring erosion. Also includes business strategy analysis (Porter/Teece frameworks applied to AI adoption) and case studies (Tesla vs Toyota).

## Relevance

The book synthesizes many threads already in the KB (cognitive offloading, desirable difficulty, performance paradox, novice vulnerability, capacity erosion) into a single organizational framework. Its primary contribution to the KB is (1) the "augmentation illusion" as a named framing for performance-competence decoupling, (2) the Dreyfus model disruption analysis showing where AI amplifies vs. where it creates ceilings, and (3) the ACEI as a proposed measurement approach. However, most core ideas are already represented in the KB through empirical sources. The book is largely theoretical synthesis with limited primary empirical data.

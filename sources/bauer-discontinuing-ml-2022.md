# Bauer et al. (2022) - Discontinuing ML Support

## Citation

Bauer, K., Nofer, M., & Abdel-Karim, B.M. (2022). The Effects of Discontinuing Machine Learning Decision Support. *Social Science Research Network*.

**DOI:** 10.2139/ssrn.4299664

## Type

Paper (experimental)

## Key Insight

ML decision aids can latently prevent development of decision-making skills—only apparent when the system is discontinued. The degree of "blind trust" in predictions determines the performance drop when AI is removed.

## Key Findings

- Skill loss only visible after ML aid removal
- Blind trust correlates with larger performance drop
- Prevention of skill development, not just atrophy
- Dependency forms during use, reveals itself after

## Relevance

Experimental evidence for [[cognitive-debt]] accumulation during AI use. Shows the hidden nature of erosion—capability loss is masked until AI is unavailable.

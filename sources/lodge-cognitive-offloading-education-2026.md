# Lodge & Loble — Cognitive Offloading and Education (2026)

## Citation

Lodge, J. M. and Loble, L. (2026). Artificial intelligence, cognitive offloading and implications for education. University of Technology Sydney. doi:10.71741/4pyxmbnjaq.31302475.

## Type

Paper (report for the Australian Network for Quality Digital Education)

## Key Insight

Distinguishes beneficial cognitive offloading (freeing working memory by outsourcing extraneous load) from detrimental cognitive offloading (bypassing the intrinsic cognitive effort that builds durable knowledge). Identifies a "performance paradox" where AI boosts immediate task performance while undermining long-term learning — driven by fluency-induced illusion of competence and metacognitive laziness.

## Relevance

Provides a cognitive-architecture grounding (Cognitive Load Theory) for concerns this KB tracks under capacity erosion, cognitive offloading, and novice vulnerability. Synthesises recent empirical evidence (Bastani et al. 2025, Fan et al. 2024, Hong et al. 2025) into a coherent framework. Introduces the beneficial/detrimental offloading distinction that maps directly onto the SCAN framework's zone-based risk model. Also identifies a "metacognitive equity gap" — AI risks disproportionately affecting learners who already lack domain knowledge and self-regulation skills.

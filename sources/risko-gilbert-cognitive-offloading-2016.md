# Risko & Gilbert (2016) - Cognitive Offloading

## Citation

Risko, E. F., & Gilbert, S. J. (2016). Cognitive offloading. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 20(9), 676-688.

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.tics.2016.07.002](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2016.07.002)

## Type

Paper (review)

## Key Insight

Humans naturally offload cognitive tasks to external tools and environments. This is adaptive when it frees resources for higher-order thinking, but can be maladaptive when it prevents the development or maintenance of internal capabilities.

## Relevance

Foundational research for understanding how AI changes cognitive work. Establishes that offloading is a natural human behavior that becomes more powerful and subtle with AI.

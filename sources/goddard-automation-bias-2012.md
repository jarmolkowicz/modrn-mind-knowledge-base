# Goddard et al. (2012) - Automation Bias Systematic Review

## Citation

Goddard, K., Roudsari, A., & Wyatt, J. C. (2012). Automation bias: A systematic review of frequency, effect mediators, and mitigators. *Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association*, 19(1), 121-127.

**DOI:** [10.1136/amiajnl-2011-000089](https://doi.org/10.1136/amiajnl-2011-000089)

## Type

Paper (systematic review)

## Key Insight

Systematic review confirming automation bias occurs across domains. Found that expertise level, workload, and trust calibration affect susceptibility. Importantly, even experts exhibit automation bias under certain conditions.

## Relevance

Provides evidence that automation bias affects everyone, not just novices. Relevant for understanding why even experienced professionals can over-rely on AI.
